Part 1: SMIL animation

Develop an SVG document which has a small SMIL animation of any sort (changing the geometry or size of an ellipse, or changing the blur of a bitmap, or changing the clip-path applied to a colorful collage of rectangles). After the animation runs some fixed number of iterations (you can choose a number n between 1 and 6, depending on what seems "best" to you, and set repeatCount equal to n) then it terminates. At that time it activates a script that changes the color(s) of some feature (and perhaps other aspects) of your content (either randomly or deterministically) and restarts the animation.

Part 2: Interesting SVG examples

Find three interesting examples of the usage of SVG on the Web. Offer a brief description of each and their URL. Let me know if I can share your recommendations with others.

Part 3(B): Practical SVG

Use SVG to illustrate some data (textual or numeric) that has been:

  1. provided from a web site,
  2. (or) contained in an XML or plaintext document on a server
  3. (or) bundled as an array or other JavaScript object into your SVG document.

Consider using any of the conventional ways of that illustrating data (charts, figures, graphs, maps. etc.) or consider more experimental ways of visualizing data such as shown in the work of Edward Tufte or in some of the illustrations of similarity using OWL. Hopefully, your illustration will help guide insights about the data that might not be apparent to someone just looking at the raw textual or numeric data.
